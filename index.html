 Fire safety is incredibly important because fires can cause devastating damage, injury, and loss of life. Fires can start quickly and spread rapidly, leaving little time for escape. A fire can destroy a home or business in just minutes, leaving the occupants with nothing. In addition to the physical damage, fires can cause emotional trauma, financial loss, and prolonged displacement.

 By following fire safety guidelines, you can help prevent fires from starting and minimize their impact if they do occur. Taking fire safety seriously and following established guidelines and procedures, you can help protect yourself, your loved ones, and your property from the devastating effects of fires.


FIRE SAFETY REMINDERS

Keep flammable materials away from heat sources		 Keep flammable materials such as curtains, blankets, and papers away from heat sources such as stoves, heaters, and electrical appliances. Be especially careful with items that can catch fire easily.Also, Be cautious with candles. Candles can be a fire hazard, so use them cautiously. Keep them away from anything that could catch fire, and never leave them unattended.

Be careful when cooking		 Cooking is the leading cause of house fires, so be extra careful when cooking. Never leave food unattended on the stove, and always keep flammable materials away from the heat source. If a pan catches fire while cooking, a lid can be used to smother the flames. Keep a lid nearby when cooking to be prepared for such an emergency.

Turn off electrical appliances when not in use		 When you're not using electrical appliances, make sure to turn them off and unplug them. Faulty wiring and overloaded circuits are common causes of electrical fires. Overloading electrical outlets can cause them to overheat and start a fire. Make sure to use power strips and extension cords safely and avoid plugging too many appliances into the same outlet.

Stay alert and prepared		 Make sure that you and your family have a plan in place for evacuating your home in case of a fire. Practice the plan regularly, and make sure that everyone knows what to do. Above all, stay alert and prepared for the possibility of a fire. Keep a fire extinguisher, list of emergency hotlines and a phone nearby in case of an emergency.
